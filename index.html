About Me

I recently completed my Ph.D. in Applied Mathematics and Computational Science at the University of Pennsylvania, where I developed novel techniques for modeling physical systems driven out of equilibrium under the supervision of Prof. Celia Reina and Prof. Prashant K. Purohit. Prior to that, I studied mathematics and physics at Johns Hopkins University with a particular focus on biological physics, condensed matter, and differential equations. There, I got to work under Prof. Brian Camley studying the impact of cell membrane geometry on cell motility and protein binding.
